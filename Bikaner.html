JUNAGADH FORT

No enemy could ever conquer this fort. Built in 1588 AD by Raja Raisingh, the most distinguished general of Emperor Akbar, this fort has been a center of attraction for tourists. The magnificent palaces, courtyards, balconies, umbrella-like pavilions and windows built in red sandstone and marble reflect its amazing art.

Location📍 🗺️: 28C9+Q5Q, Bikaner Fort, Bikaner, Rajasthan 334001

RAMPURIYA HAVELI

Rampuriya was a prosperous and very famous family here. The eyes of those who see this mansion remain wide open in astonishment. Made of red stone, the beautifully carved, thin and strong pillars, windows, arched doors, fluted lattices, divankhanas and cellars are amazing. The flowers, leaves and golden workmanship used in the decoration of the windows mesmerize the spectators. Hotel Bhanwar Niwas is currently being run by the Rampuriya family in this mansion.

Location📍 🗺️:Rampuria Havellis, Joshiwara, Old Bikaner, Bikaner, Rajasthan

DESHNOKE - KARNI MATA TEMPLE

The temple which has made its mark for rats all over the world, is 30 km from Bikaner. Is in Deshnok village, at a distance of . It is famous among tourists as 'Temple of Rats'. There are about 25,000 black rats here, which are called 'Kaba'. Their sanctity is evident from the fact that if a rat dies after being crushed by someone's feet, then a silver rat has to be made and offered at the feet of Karni Mata. According to a legend, Karni Mata's stepson Lakshmana died by drowning in a pond. Karni Mata prayed to Yamraj to bring her back to life. Yamraj refused and then agreed on the condition that from that day onwards all the descendants of Lakshman and Karni Mata would live in the form of rats. Considering thousands of rats as sacred, devotees offer laddus and large plates of milk in the temple, which the rats eat and drink.

Location📍 🗺️: NH 89, Deshnok, Rajasthan 334801

GAJNER PALACE AND LAKE

Gajner is an incomparable jewel of Thar. Gajner Palace was founded by Maharaja Gaj Singh Ji of Bikaner in 1784, and then completed by the great Maharaja Ganga Singh of Bikaner on the banks of the lake. It was a hunting ground and resting place for the royal family as well as guests. Now it has been converted into a hotel.

Location📍 🗺️: Gajner, Rajasthan 334301

KATARIASAR VILLAGE

On Jaipur Road, this village has a very rich rural and cultural life. Looking at the sand dunes in Katariasar and the light of the evening sun falling on them, it seems as if gold is scattered all over the earth. Tourists come here in large numbers and get mesmerized by watching 'Jasnath ji's fire dance' on the sand banks. Herds of desert fox, rabbit, chinkara, peacock, pheasant, quail and chakor are seen here. 45 km from Bikaner. Katariasar is a clean and prosperous village. Many eateries and restaurants have also opened here.

Location📍 🗺️: Katriyasar, Bikaner, Rajasthan

KOLAYAT

An important pilgrimage place for Hindus, where devotees from far and wide come every year to visit the temple. Kolayat is a sacred lake, which is about 50 km from Bikaner. Is at a distance. The history here tells the story of Kapil Muni, the pioneer of Sankhya Yoga, who was so overwhelmed by the peaceful atmosphere of this place that he interrupted his journey to the northwest and, in order to attain freedom from the cycle of samsara, meditated here. started doing. The temples, ghats and sacred lakes located here invite devotees. The markets here are liked by tourists.

Location📍 🗺️:SRI KOLAYAT JI, BIKANER, RAJASTHAN, 334302.

LALGARH PALACE AND MUSEUM

Maharaja Ganga Singh had built this grand palace in 1902 AD, in the memory of his late father Maharaja Lal Singh. Its architectural design was conceptualized by 'Sir Swinton Jacob', an English architect. The palace is built entirely of red sandstone and is a blend of Rajputana, Islamic and European architecture. Presently this palace has been converted into a heritage hotel. Some part serves as the residence of the royal family of Bikaner. Shri Sadul Museum is in a part of this palace.

Location📍 🗺️:D/68, Samta Nagar, Bikaner, Beechhwal Rural, Rajasthan
